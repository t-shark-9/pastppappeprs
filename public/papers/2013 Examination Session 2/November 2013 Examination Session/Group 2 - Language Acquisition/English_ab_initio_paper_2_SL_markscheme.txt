3.   You have just returned from a visit to your English correspondent’s family. You write
     to thank them for their welcome and invite them to visit you. You explain what you
     enjoyed most in their country and what they will see and do when they visit your
     country. Write the email.

     Criterion A: Language (maximum [8 marks])

     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Does the candidate use a range of vocabulary and grammatical structures?
     Vocabulary: tourism; food; weather.
     Grammatical structures: short, simple sentences.
     Tenses: the present and the past.

     Does the candidate show an awareness of the appropriate register for the task?
     Register: semi-formal.

     Criterion B: Message (maximum [8 marks])

     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Are the ideas well-developed?
     Ideas: relevant, comprehensive, some details.

     Is there evidence of a logical structure?
     Introduction: reference to the stay / welcome and an explicit “thank you”.

     Development (2 or 3 paragraphs):
     • first paragraph: thank you and invitation
     • second paragraph: what you enjoyed most in their country
     • third paragraph: what they will see and do in your country.

     Conclusion: this may be written in a variety of ways.

     Are cohesive devices used effectively?
     Cohesive devices: first, next …; also, moreover …

     Criterion C: Format (maximum [2 marks])

     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Does the candidate use the appropriate format?
     Text type – Informal letter: date, greetings, final formula, signature.

4.   As class representative you send a leaflet to all parents from your school inviting them to
     attend an International Day at the school. Describe the programme for the day:
     mention some of the activities. Explain why this day is important for the students and
     parents. Write the text of the leaflet.

     Criterion A: Language (maximum [8 marks])

     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Does the candidate use a range of vocabulary and grammatical structures?
     Vocabulary: education; internationalism.
     Grammatical structures: short, simple sentences.
     Tenses: principally the present and the future.

     Does the candidate show an awareness of the appropriate register for the task?
     Register: formal or semi-formal.

     Criterion B: Message (maximum [8 marks])

     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Are the ideas well-developed?
     Ideas: relevant, some details.

     Is there evidence of a logical structure?
     Introduction: purpose of the leaflet: invitation to international day.

     Development (2 or 3 paragraphs):
     • details of the day’s activities
     • explanation of why it is important to support this event/why the event is important.

     Conclusion: this may be the expression of encouragement to attend.

     Are cohesive devices used effectively?
     Cohesive devices: first, next …; also, therefore …

     Criterion C: Format (maximum [2 marks])

     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Does the candidate use the appropriate format?
     Text type – Leaflet: title, subtitles/bullet-points.

5.   Every month your I.B. English class posts messages on a blog for a class in an English
     speaking country. This month your teacher has suggested that you write about the
     educational system in your country. Write the blog entry describing your country’s
     school system.

     Criterion A: Language (maximum [8 marks])

     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Does the candidate use a range of vocabulary and grammatical structures?
     Vocabulary: education.
     Grammatical structures: short, simple sentences: a variety of tenses principally the present
     indicative.

     Does the candidate show an awareness of the appropriate register for the task?
     Register: semi-formal.

     Criterion B: Message (maximum [8 marks])
     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Are the ideas well-developed?
     Ideas: relevant.

     Is there evidence of a logical structure?
     Introduction: introduces the topic of education.
     Development: details of educational system.
     Conclusion: this will vary from candidate to candidate.

     Are cohesive devices used effectively?
     Cohesive devices: first, next …; also, moreover …; on the other hand, however …

     Criterion C: Format (maximum [2 marks])
     Please refer to the Language ab initio guide (March 2011).

     Does the candidate use the appropriate format?
     Text type – Blog: Form of address / opening greeting(s), form of closure / concluding
     remarks.
