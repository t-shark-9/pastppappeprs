                                              Section A

Answer one question from this section. Each question is worth [20 marks].

Hinduism

1.   Discuss the significance of the Vedas in Hindu belief and practice.


2.   Examine the relationship between God (Brahman) and self (atman) in Hindu thought.



Buddhism

3.   Assess the importance of the Three Jewels to lay Buddhists.


4.   Examine the meaning and importance of the principle of karma within Buddhist ethics.



Sikhism

5.   Examine the link between Sikh ritual life and Khalsa membership.


6.   Assess the degree to which the emphasis on family life (kirat karni and vand ke chakna) forms the
     foundation of Sikh ethics.



Open-ended question

7.   With reference to one religion, either Hinduism or Buddhism or Sikhism, discuss the importance
     of overcoming clinging to the “self”.

Judaism

8.      To what extent do you agree with the statement that “Judaism teaches that abortion is
        always wrong”?

Christianity

Islam
