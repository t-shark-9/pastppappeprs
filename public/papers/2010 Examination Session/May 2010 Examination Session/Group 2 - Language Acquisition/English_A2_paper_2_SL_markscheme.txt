These notes to examiners are intended only as guidelines to assist marking. They are not offered as
an exhaustive and fixed set of responses or approaches to which all answers must rigidly adhere.
Good ideas or angles not offered here should be acknowledged and rewarded as appropriate.
Similarly, answers that do not include all the ideas or approaches suggested here should not be so
heavily penalized as to distort appreciation of individuality.

These questions invite the candidate to consider the Cultural Option “Language and culture”.

1.   A barely satisfactory answer will provide some reasons for its argument.          One or two
     examples will be given.

     A better answer will draw on appropriate examples in considering the idea that a
     language evolves to express the culture and experience of its users. The essay will have a
     coherent structure.

     The best answers will demonstrate a range of reasons for the arguments maintained and will
     support those arguments with examples that are pertinent, precise, and varied. The essays will
     be well organized.

These questions invite the candidate to consider a Literary Option.

8.    A barely satisfactory answer will offer a general discussion of the ways that animals are
      important in at least two works studied. It will use at least one supporting example from
      each text.

      A better answer will draw on appropriate examples to offer a reasonable discussion of the
      ways that animals are used in at least two works studied. The essay will demonstrate a good
      understanding of the texts and will have a coherent structure.

      The best answers will use a range of precise and pertinent examples to discuss ways in which
      animals are used in at least two works studied. Essays will not be restricted to a discussion
      of plot, but will discuss relevant themes. Essays will be well organized and persuasive.


9.    A barely satisfactory answer will offer a general discussion of the use and effects of contrast
      in at least two works studied. It will use at least one supporting example from each work.

      A better answer will draw on appropriate examples to offer a reasonable discussion of the use
      and effects of contrast in at least two works studied. The essay will demonstrate a good
      understanding of the texts and will have a coherent structure.

      The best answers will use a range of precise and pertinent examples to discuss the effects of
      contrast in at least two works studied. They will foreground the implications of contrast
      for theme. Essays will be well organized and persuasive.


10.   A barely satisfactory answer will offer a general discussion of the ways that justice is defined

      A better answer will draw on appropriate examples to offer a reasonable discussion of the
      ways that justice is defined in at least two works studied. The essay will demonstrate a good
      understanding of the texts and will have a coherent structure.

      The best answers will use a range of precise and pertinent examples to discuss the ways that
