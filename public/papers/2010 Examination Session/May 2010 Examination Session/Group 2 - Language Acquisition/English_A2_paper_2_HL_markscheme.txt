These notes to examiners are intended only as guidelines to assist marking. They are not offered as
an exhaustive and fixed set of responses or approaches to which all answers must rigidly adhere.
Good ideas or angles not offered here should be acknowledged and rewarded as appropriate.
Similarly, answers which do not include all the ideas or approaches suggested here should not be so
heavily penalized as to distort appreciation of individuality.

These questions invite the candidates to consider the Cultural Option “Language and culture”.

1.   A barely satisfactory answer will offer a general discussion of the idea that language is the
     “collective memory bank” of a people. One or two examples will be given.

     A better answer will draw on appropriate examples in its consideration of the idea
     that language is the “collective memory bank” of a people. The essay will have a
     coherent structure.

     The best answers will demonstrate a wide range of reasons for their arguments and will
     support those arguments with examples that are pertinent, precise, and varied. The essays will
     be well organized.


2.   A barely satisfactory answer will offer a general discussion of the idea that fewer languages
     might lead to better communication among people. One or two examples will be given.

     A better answer will draw on appropriate examples in its consideration of the idea that
     fewer languages might lead to better communication among people. The essay will have a
     coherent structure.

     The best answers will demonstrate a wide range of reasons for their arguments and will
     support those arguments with examples that are pertinent, precise, and varied. The essays will
     be well organized.

These questions invite the candidates to consider a Literary option.

8.    A barely satisfactory answer will offer a general discussion of the role that regret plays in at
      least two works studied. It will use at least one example from each text.

      A better answer will draw on appropriate evidence to make an argument about the role that
      regret plays in at least two works studied. It will address theme as well as plot and will have a
      coherent structure.

      The best answers will draw on pertinent and varied examples to make a persuasive argument
      about the role that regret plays in at least two works studied. It will discuss theme in some
      detail and will be well organized.


9.    A barely satisfactory answer will offer a general discussion of the way that a good life is
      represented in at least two works studied. It will use at least one example from each text.

      A better answer will draw on appropriate evidence to make an argument about the way that a
      good life is understood in at least two works studied. It will address theme as well as plot and
      will have a coherent structure.

      The best answers will draw on pertinent and varied examples to make a persuasive argument
      about the way that a good life is understood in at least two works studied. It will discuss
      theme in some detail and will be well organized.
