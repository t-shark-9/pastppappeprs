1.   You play in a school music group. You are going to give a concert to collect money for a
     charity. Write the text of a poster to advertise the concert. Explain:
        what the event is
        the choice of the charity
        where and when the concert will take place
        how much the tickets cost
        where people can buy the tickets.

     Criterion A: Language

     Please refer to Criterion A in the guide.

     Verbs will be in the present and future tenses and imperative form.
     Grammatical structures are generally simple.
     Vocabulary is simple, relevant to a concert / charity.
     The minimum number of words has been written.

     Criterion B: Communication skills

     Please refer to Criterion B in the guide.

     Information to be given in any order:
      what the event is
      the choice of the charity
      where and when the concert will take place
      how much the tickets cost
      where people can buy the tickets.

     To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will provide information for at least three of the
     points above.

     To reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will:
      provide all the information listed above
      structure the text logically.
                                              –4–               N12/2/ANENG/SP2/ENG/TZ0/XX/M


Criterion C: Presentation

Please refer to Criterion C in the guide.

To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will use:
 one element which identifies a poster: title, sections, subtitles, short paragraphs
 a generally appropriate register: standard.
Note: linking words are not required.

To reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will use:
 sections, subtitles or short, spaced paragraphs
 the appropriate register consistently.
Note: linking words are not required.

If the text is not a poster, the mark for Criterion C will not be higher than [6 marks].
To be considered a poster, there must be a title.

2.   It is going to be your grandmother’s birthday. Unfortunately, you cannot go to the
     celebration. You send her a birthday card with a message to accompany the present you
     have chosen for her. In the message:
      wish her a happy birthday
      explain why you cannot come to her birthday celebration
      explain why you chose this present for her
      say when you will see her again.

     Criterion A: Language

     Please refer to Criterion A in the guide.

     Verbs will be in the present and future tenses.
     Grammatical structures are simple. Sentences are short.
     Vocabulary is simple, relevant to celebrations / presents.
     The minimum number of words has been written.

     Criterion B: Communication skills

     Please refer to Criterion B in the guide.

     Information to be given in any order:
      wish her a happy birthday
      explain why you cannot come to her birthday celebration
      explain why you chose this present for her
      say when you will see her again.

     To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will provide information for at least two of the
     points above.

     To reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will:
      provide all the information listed above
      include some details
      organize the information in a logical / clear order.

     Criterion C: Presentation

     Please refer to Criterion C in the guide.

     To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will use:
      one or several characteristics of a message: opening greetings, closing greetings, signature
      a generally appropriate register: standard.

     The reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will use:
      at least two characteristics of a message: opening greetings, closing greetings, signature
      a consistent register: standard.

     If the text is not a message, the mark for Criterion C will not be higher than [6 marks].
     To be considered a message, there must be a greeting and signature.

3.   You interview the new headmaster/headmistress of your school for the school magazine.
     You want to know about his/her past work, why he/she has chosen to come and work in
     this school and what changes he/she wants to make in the school. Write the text of
     the interview.

     Criterion A: Language

     Please refer to Criterion A in the guide.

     Verbs will be in the past, present and future tenses.
     Grammatical structures are generally simple.
     Vocabulary is relevant to school / education / work.
     The minimum number of words has been reached.

     Criterion B: Communication skills

     Please refer to Criterion B in the guide.

     Information to be given in any order:
      his/her past work
      why he/she has chosen to come and work in this school
      what changes he/she wants to make in the school.

     To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will provide information for at least one of the
     points above.

     To reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will:
      give all the information listed above
      include at least two examples to illustrate the points of information
      present the information clearly / logically.

     Criterion C: Presentation

     Please refer to Criterion C in the guide.

     To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will use:
      one characteristic of an interview: title, introduction, question–answer format
      a generally appropriate register: standard or formal
      simple linking words.

     To reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will use:
      two characteristics of an interview
      a consistent register: standard or formal
      a variety of linking words.

     If the text is not an interview, the mark for Criterion C will not be higher than [6 marks].
     To be considered an interview, there must be at least one question and answer.

4.   You have entered a writing competition. The title of your essay is “My story, my
     history”. You have to write about your great-grandparents and your grandparents:
     where they lived, what they did, what you know about them.

     Criterion A: Language

     Please refer to Criterion A in the guide.

     Verbs will be in the past and present tenses.
     Grammatical structures are simple.
     Vocabulary is linked to the family.
     The minimum number of words has been written.

     Criterion B: Communication skills

     Please refer to Criterion B in the guide.

     Information to be given in any order:
      your great grandparents: where they lived, what they did, what you know about them
      your grandparents: where they lived, what they did, what you know about them.

     To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will mention at least one generation of his/her
     family.

     To reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will:
      mention both generations
      give supporting detail/examples.

     Criterion C: Presentation

     Please refer to Criterion C in the guide.

     To reach markbands [5 to 8] the candidate will use:
      paragraphs
      a first person point of view
      a generally appropriate register: standard or formal
      some simple linking words.

     To reach markbands [9 to 10] the candidate will use:
      a title
      paragraphs
      a first person point of view
      a consistent register: standard or formal
      a variety of linking words.

     If the text is not an essay, the mark for Criterion C will not be higher than [6 marks].
     To be considered an essay, there must be paragraphs.
