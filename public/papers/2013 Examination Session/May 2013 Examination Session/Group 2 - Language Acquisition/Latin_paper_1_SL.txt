But it is foolish of us to dare to compare Drusus, Africanus, Pompeius, or ourselves,
with Publius Clodius. All these things were endurable. The death of Publius Clodius no
one can bear with equanimity. The senate is in mourning; the knights grieve; the whole
state is broken down as if with age; the municipalities are in mourning; the colonies
are bowed down; the very fields even regret so beneficent, so useful, so kind-hearted
a citizen!
